Recovery Stories

For a time, I debated with the thought of seeing a psychotherapist and asking for help. After all, I've seen a psychiatrist before and it wasn't a pleasant experience. But in April, after endless nights of crying, I finally asked my mom to take me to Interspect. I came across Interspect through Psychology Matters Asia. I chose to go here because I thought it was more accessible. Plus, I liked that they offered Art Therapy and the therapists were women. I believed that I could open up more to someone whom I could think of as a mother figure. The sessions were life-changing. Although it has been months since I last saw Ms. Cherrie, those weeks I've talked to her were actually good. I felt like I was a different person after every session. Ms. Cherrie is empathic, kind and patient. She also pushed me to do things I wouldn't normally do. She reminded me of my strengths, and the fact that having weaknesses is not a terrible thing. Most of all, Ms. Cherrie instilled in me a sense of ambition: that I can do anything if I put my mind into it. I'm still struggling with life, and that's okay. Somehow, meeting up with Ms. Cherrie allowed me to grow up and think of the bigger picture: that it's okay to feel pain, and that it, too, shall pass. Thanks, Ms. Cherrie. Hope to see you again soon.

- Ysa Navarro
